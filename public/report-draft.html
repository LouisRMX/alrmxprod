How each scenario is calculated

Today, 51% Operational, 64% Target, 70%
3,242 m³/day ÷ 6,300 plant capacity = 51%. 87 trucks × 5.0 trips × 7.45 m³ avg load. Actual TAT: 170 min. At 135 min TAT the fleet delivers 4,031 m³/day. 4,031 ÷ 6,300 plant capacity = 64%. This is the ceiling for the current fleet at 25 km radius. No additional trucks required. 4,410 m³/day (= 6,300 × 70%) ÷ 6,300 = 70%. Requires 379 m³/day more than fleet ceiling. Achieved via: 8 trucks added, OR 8 m³ avg load, OR 21 km avg delivery radius (TAT 123 min = 6.8 trips/truck).

Reaching 70% utilisation requires modest structural adjustments beyond operational improvements. Three viable paths: 8 additional trucks (brings fleet to 95), larger average mixer load (from 7.45 m³ to ~8 m³), or shorter delivery radius (from 25 km to 21 km). The on-site assessment will establish which lever is most feasible for your operation.
